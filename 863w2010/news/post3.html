  • King J Martin Luther (1951). “The Personalism of J.M.E. McTaggart Under Criticism.” In Carson C, Holloran P, Luker R, Russell PA, ed., The Papers of Martin Luther King, Jr (Vol. 2). University of California Press. 61–76Â 
  • This appears to be a seminar paper written in his first year (?) of graduate school at Boston University, in pursuit of his PhD in systematic theology.
    • Apparently he got an A for the paper.
    • The argumentation is weak. For example, he argues that, since McTaggart is the only philosopher he knows who is both an atheist and believes in the immortality of persons, it must be that the arguments that support immortality also support theism. WTF?
    • I hope nobody ever thinks it makes sense to collect my seminar papers from the first year of graduate school. That really isn’t fair.
